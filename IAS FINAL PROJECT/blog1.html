What Is Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity means protecting computer systems, networks, and data from attacks, damage, or unauthorized access. It focuses on keeping information private, accurate, and available whenever needed.

In simple terms, cybersecurity is about defense and prevention. It ensures that your data stays safe even if someone tries to hack or disrupt your system.

The CIA Triad

This is the foundation of all cybersecurity concepts. It stands for:

  • Confidentiality - making sure only authorized people can see sensitive information.
  • Integrity - keeping data accurate and unchanged unless someone with permission edits it.
  • Availability - making sure data and systems are always accessible when needed.

Example: an online banking system must keep data private (confidentiality), ensure no one changes balances (integrity), and stay online for customers (availability).

Pillars of Cybersecurity

1. People

users, employees, and IT professionals who interact with systems. Human error is the most common cause of security breaches, so awareness and training matter a lot.

2. Process

Defines how organizations respond to threats. This includes Incident Detection, Response, Management, and Forensics to handle security events properly.

3. Technology

Involves the tools that protect information, such as firewalls, encryption, antivirus, and intrusion detection systems.

Common Cyber Threats

  • Phishing - fake emails or messages designed to steal your information.
  • Malware - software that harms computers, like viruses, worms, and ransomware.
  • Social Engineering - tricking people into revealing sensitive data.
  • Insider Threats - employees or trusted users misusing access, accidentally or intentionally.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use strong and unique passwords.
  • Turn on two-factor authentication whenever possible.
  • Keep software updated to patch vulnerabilities.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or attachments.
  • Backup your files regularly.
  • Stay informed about common scams and attacks.

Cybersecurity is a shared responsibility. Even small habits can make a huge difference in preventing data breaches.
